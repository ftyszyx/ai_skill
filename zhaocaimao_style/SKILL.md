# 招财猫风格

Use this skill to turn market notes, trading journals, or daily热点复盘 into a sharp, colloquial Chinese公众号稿. Capture the reusable writing mechanics of the reference article, not the author's exact voice or original wording.

## Ground Rules

- Verify date-sensitive market data, news, prices, and policy items before writing when they are central to the piece.
- Do not fabricate涨跌幅、成交额、公告金额、时间线、持仓动作, or named company facts.
- Do not copy reference-article sentences, catchphrases, paragraph order, or distinctive insults. Write an original article using the style pattern.
- Keep financial compliance in mind: frame as个人记录、市场观察、复盘, and add a short non-advice disclaimer when the output is publishable.
- Preserve the user's own trading decision and risk attitude. Do not turn a cautious author into a荐股号.

## Style Profile

Voice:
- First-person, direct, fast, conversational.
- Opinionated but not pretending to be omniscient.
- Uses simple spoken Chinese, short paragraphs, and occasional blunt judgments.
- Treats the reader as already familiar with basic market language.

Tone:
- High-energy opening, then calmer explanation.
- Willing to say "看不懂", "没答案", "我不站队", "这钱我认了" when uncertainty exists.
- Uses controlled slang sparingly; one punchy line per section is enough.
- Avoids official-report language, long qualifiers, and academic transitions.

Paragraph rhythm:
- Mostly 1-3 sentences per paragraph.
- Start with a strong market observation or emotional temperature.
- Follow with hard facts or numbers.
- Then explain the mechanism: why money moved, who benefited, who was left behind.
- End each segment with a personal judgment or risk boundary.

## Article Structure

Use this default structure unless the user's draft clearly needs another order:

1. **Opening heat check**
   - One punchy sentence summarizing the day's market emotion.
   - 2-4 concrete facts: sector涨跌、代表公司、指数表现、外部 news.
   - Explain the trigger.

2. **Main line**
   - Pick the strongest market line, usually the one with业绩、产业趋势、资金虹吸, or global映射.
   - Explain why the move is not just "炒概念".
   - Name the biggest tension: valuation, crowding, supply bottleneck, policy risk, or cyclical top.

3. **Opposite side**
   - Use跷跷板 logic: if money is chasing one line, which old line is being drained.
   - Contrast growth vs old economy, AI vs consumption, risk appetite vs defense, or theme vs fundamentals.
   - Avoid making both sides sound equally attractive unless the facts support it.

4. **Personal position**
   - Insert the author's own trade, holding, regret, or non-action.
   - Explain the original reason for buying/holding and whether today's move changed that reason.
   - Make the decision sound accountable, not heroic.

5. **Numbered quick hits**
   - Add 2-4 short numbered items for secondary热点.
   - Each item should include: event, why it matters, likely market effect, risk caveat.
   - Keep each item 1-3 paragraphs.

6. **Closing**
   - End with a short personal line, preferably about discipline, risk, or not overreaching.
   - Add a compliance line if publishing: "仅为个人记录，不构成投资建议。"

## Writing Moves

Use these moves to create the feel:

- **Fact then slap:** give a number, then one plainspoken interpretation.
- **Mechanism bridge:** "表面看是 X，实际上是 Y."
- **资金流 lens:** "钱不是消失了，是从 A 挪到了 B."
- **No answer posture:** state uncertainty directly instead of forcing a prediction.
- **Personal cost accounting:** "赚到了就认，卖飞也认；原始逻辑变了就走。"
- **Cycle humility:** separate short-term emotion from medium-term industry logic.

## Avoid

- Do not write as if recommending specific stocks.
- Do not overuse vulgarity, shouting, or internet catchphrases.
- Do not mention "模仿某某作者" in the output.
- Do not pad with宏大叙事 when a concrete price, announcement, or trade decision is available.
- Do not make every paragraph sarcastic; the style needs facts first, attitude second.

## Output Defaults

For full rewrites, produce:

- A title with market temperature, not a marketing headline.
- A publishable正文 around the requested length.
- Optional image-placement notes only when the user asks for配图 or排版.
- A final disclaimer if the article discusses stocks, positions, or trading decisions.

For partial edits, preserve the user's original facts and section intent, and only change rhythm, wording, ordering, and transitions.
